Research at STRIDE Lab

Our research sits at the intersection of causality, symbolic AI, and deep learning — building AI systems that are not just accurate, but explainable, robust, and aligned with human reasoning.

Core framework

Causal Neuro-Symbolic AI (CausalNeSy)

CausalNeSy is our flagship framework that embeds causal knowledge into neural systems. Traditional deep learning excels at pattern recognition but struggles with why things happen — CausalNeSy bridges this gap by integrating causal graphs and structural causal models with neural architectures.

This approach enables counterfactual reasoning ("what would have happened if…"), intervention analysis, and transparent explanations — crucial for high-stakes domains like healthcare, manufacturing, and autonomous systems.

Structured knowledge

Knowledge Graphs & Ontologies

Knowledge Graphs provide a machine-readable scaffold of facts and relationships that neural models can leverage for more grounded reasoning. At STRIDE Lab, we build domain-specific KGs and ontologies that enable semantic interoperability and causal enrichment across diverse applications.

Our work spans autonomous driving (CausalKG), industrial digital twins (MetaverseKG), and biomedical ontologies — connecting symbolic knowledge to neural inference pipelines.

Real-world impact

AI for Healthcare

One of the most critical domains for Causal AI is healthcare — where understanding why a patient's condition changes, not just predicting it, is essential for actionable clinical decision support.

Our work focuses on pediatric asthma prediction, personalized treatment effect modeling, and transparent explanations for black-box clinical models. We align with NIH and NSF priorities around health equity and personalized medicine.

Our causal framework enables physicians to ask interventional questions — "if we change medication X, what is the likely outcome for this specific patient?" — a level of reasoning beyond standard predictive models.
